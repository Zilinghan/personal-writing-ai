Awards — Best Paper Award, 5th DataCV Workshop at CVPR 2026

The award certificate is provided as [award]-datacv.pdf. Additional context about this award:

The DataCV 2026 Workshop, officially titled the 5th DataCV Workshop and Challenge, was held in conjunction with CVPR 2026, one of the world's leading international conferences on computer vision. The workshop took place on June 3, 2026, in Denver, Colorado, USA.

For the paper track, submissions from researchers worldwide were evaluated through a double-blind review process by experts from academia and industry. The workshop accepted 26 papers in total across oral and poster presentations. The Best Paper Award was selected from accepted papers based on technical quality, novelty, significance, clarity, and relevance to the workshop theme. The award was decided by the workshop organizers and program committee, whose members include researchers from institutions such as Australian National University, Stanford University, Leiden University, UC San Diego, Google Research, University of Science and Technology Beijing, Shandong University, and AMD, among others. One recipient was selected, and the award was presented during the DataCV 2026 Workshop award ceremony.

Public information about the workshop, accepted papers, program, organizers, and committee members is available at: https://sites.google.com/view/datacv-2026-cvpr/

The Trillion Parameter Consortium (TPC) is a global, community-driven open initiative at the intersection of AI and high-performance computing, currently comprising 82 member organizations spanning academia, national laboratories, industry, funding agencies, and venture capital firms worldwide. Its mission is to advance responsible, large-scale AI development for scientific and engineering applications. TPC convenes AI leaders across sectors annually to establish best practices for enterprise-scale AI development; TPC25 was held in San Jose, CA in July 2025.

The American Medical Informatics Association (AMIA) is the leading professional society for biomedical and health informatics in the United States, founded in 1988 and representing more than 5,000 members from 42 countries. The AMIA 2025 Informatics Summit, held in Pittsburgh, PA in March 2025, brought together researchers, clinicians, data scientists, and industry professionals across translational bioinformatics, precision medicine, clinical research informatics, and AI — making it the premier national venue for advances at the intersection of AI and clinical medicine.
